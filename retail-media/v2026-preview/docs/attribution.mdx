Open Auction campaigns are performance based.  As such, it is primarily evaluated by advertisers on its ability to drive sales at an efficient Return On Ad Spend (ROAS).  While we offer certain flexibility in the choice of attribution rules, we will always enforce that the user that made the sale was the same as the one that interacted with the ad.  Similarly, we will enforce that there is a connection between the purchased product and the advertised one.

# User Matching

The Criteo Retail Media Platform will rely on four user identifiers to determine whether a purchase is related to your ad(s) or not:

* The Retailer’s Criteo Retail Media ID
* The Retailer’s 1st Party Cookie ID
* The Criteo Cookie ID
* The Criteo X-Device ID

All of our user matching rules are purely deterministic.  We never use IP addresses or probabilistic models to determine attribution at the user level.

# SKU Matching

The Criteo Retail Media Platform considers SKUs as attributable if:

* The exact same SKU than the one promoted in your ad is purchased by a Shopper you’ve reached. We call this as “Same SKU attribution”.
* One of the SKU that is within the same category and Brand than the promoted one, is purchased by a Shopper you’ve reached. We call this as “Same Category attribution”.

# Attribution Windows

The conversion cycle can be drastically different between a Shopper buying groceries and a Shopper buying a TV. Your ROAS measurement can also be different depending on your objectives (lower or upper funnel) and the type of ads that you are running.

As a result, in order to give you more flexibility, you can choose from the following options when setting up your campaign attribution model:

* Post-click: 7, 14, 30 days
* Post-view: None, 1, 7, 14, 30 days

While these settings are made at the campaign level, the Reporting API can retrieve data from all supported combinations of attribution window settings.

Note that the post-view setting cannot exceed the post-click one.  Additionally, Criteo attributes conversions to the date of the conversion, not the date of the marketing activity.

# Deduplication

In order to provide you with the more accurate view of attribution as possible, our attribution model takes a deduplication mechanism into account.

This mechanism ensures that a purchase is only attributed once to a campaign even if multiple clicks or impressions were eligible to attribution.

Our model use prioritization rules to determine which advertising event of your ad is the most legitimate to be the attributable event. In descending order of priority, we apply the following rules:

* Clicks take precedence over impressions
* Same SKU matches take precedence over Same Category matches
* If SKU matching and user matching yield the same characteristics, then events that are the closest to the purchase time take precedence

Deduplication takes place at the campaign level. This means that two campaigns could potentially attribute the same purchase from the same user. In order to prevent this, you can run all of your similar or related marketing efforts in the same campaign and simply use different line items.

Note that media outside of Criteo Retail Media Platform are not taken into account within the deduplication and attribution methodology

# Assists

In the case where multiple ad events qualify for the same attributed sale, we want to keep track of the fact that they still contributed to the user's journey. We chose to do this via the concept of Assists.

An assist is given to a line item if this line item was on the path to purchase of the user but did not get credit for the sale that occurred. For example it will transcribe the fact that although a particular line item was not necessarily the last to interact before a sale, it made contributions along the way. This is typically valuable to show the usefulness and synergies of branding or offsite media in conjunction with traditional auction ads line items.

All assist computations are done internally within a single campaign and according to this campaign's attribution settings.
